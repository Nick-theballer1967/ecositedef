Building performance economics, assessed before you invest

EcoAI helps building owners and operators understand whether continuous optimisation is likely to create a measurable commercial return before progressing to technical validation or implementation.

Our approach starts with the economics of the building, not the technology. Using basic building and energy information, EcoAI prepares a Desktop Commercial Assessment to establish the indicative financial opportunity, likely carbon benefit and whether the building presents a strong enough case to progress.

Commercial value first

Not every building presents the same level of opportunity. EcoAI assesses energy spend, building use, HVAC profile, BMS presence, operating patterns and remote access route before deciding whether further technical work is justified.

Building economics

Review annual energy consumption, tariff context and likely HVAC-driven cost reduction before moving into technical detail.

Operational profile

Progress decision

Decide whether the opportunity is credible enough to justify deeper technical validation and business case development.

Desktop Commercial Assessment

What the Desktop Commercial Assessment shows

The Desktop Commercial Assessment gives owners and operators an early view of whether continuous optimisation could create a commercially credible opportunity.

  • Indicative annual savings
  • Estimated annual financial benefit
  • Indicative carbon reduction
  • Commercial attractiveness
  • Remote access gateway status
  • Recommendation to progress, pause or stop

Commercial routes

Two routes to a positive business case

Subscription model

A low-capital route designed to be funded from savings delivered.

Capital purchase

A capital investment route for stronger long-term net benefit, lower ongoing service cost and clear payback.

Technical validation

Technical validation before implementation

Where the desktop commercial case is strong, EcoAI reviews the existing BMS, remote access route, control strategy, available data points and HVAC capability. myCoreAI is introduced once the commercial opportunity and technical route are clear. The existing BMS remains the control system.

BMS and data access

Confirm the BMS platform, available trend data, control points and remote access route.

Control strategy

Review how plant is currently controlled and where selected HVAC setpoints can be optimised safely.

Implementation route

Define how optimisation would be delivered while keeping existing control logic and operational responsibility in place.

Business case development

Building Performance Business Case

Expected value

Estimated energy, cost and carbon benefit.

Commercial model

Route to delivery

Implementation steps, technical requirements and verification approach.
