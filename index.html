Challenges for commercial real estate

As energy regulations evolve, there is uncertainty about future requirements and the costs associated with compliance. Teams need to follow regulations while staying attractive to tenants.

High energy costs

Volatility in energy prices must be handled sustainably to protect NOI and keep building operations stable.

Staying attractive to tenants

Tenants are already requiring higher energy efficiency standards with specific requirements on the indoor climate.

Diverse technical platforms

Digitalisation takes longer due to a lack of standardisation across the technical platforms found in real estate portfolios.

Autonomous building optimisation

Our core solution, myCoreAI, reads data from buildings as well as external sources like weather and social data, and creates a unique algorithm to optimize each building according to that building’s specific conditions.

Parameters

The optimization works a little like if a human did it, the big difference being the number of parameters used and that myCoreAI sends optimized signals to the steering system every 15 mins, a human would generally do it far less often than that.

Why MyCoreAI BMS Optimisation Works

MyCoreAI works because it improves the gaps left by traditional Building Management System (BMS) control. Most BMS strategies rely on fixed schedules and static tuning, which struggle to adapt to real-world changes like weather, occupancy patterns, and seasonal drift.

Continuous optimisation on top of your existing BMS

MyCoreAI sits alongside the existing BMS and continuously identifies the lowest-energy way to maintain comfort and indoor air quality. By analysing live building data and outcomes, it makes small, safe adjustments at regular intervals to keep performance optimised over time.

  • Adapts to real building behaviour, not assumptions
  • Reduces wasted energy from overheating, over-ventilation, and simultaneous heating/cooling
  • Maintains comfort and compliance using defined constraints
  • Prevents performance drift by optimising continuously, not occasionally
  • Provides clear, auditable evidence of savings and performance

The result is sustained energy reduction while protecting (and often improving) comfort—without replacing systems or disrupting building users.
